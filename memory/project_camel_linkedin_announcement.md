Becca published a LinkedIn post on 2026-07-17 announcing the CAMEL NSF award ($1.5M), linking to the NSF SBE announcement (nsf.gov/sbe/updates/nsf-invests-75m-across-5-projects-enhance-k-12-mathematics). Full text is saved in the repo at `grant/communications/2026-07-17-linkedin-award-announcement.md` (committed and pushed to origin/main).

Key framing decisions worth remembering for future CAMEL comms:
- Core hook: K-12 curricula teach on clean/tidy datasets, but real industrial/research data is high-dimensional, noisy, incomplete — kids need exposure to that ambiguity before they enter the STEM workforce.
- Becca rejected a draft closing line ("we have to stop protecting students from that ambiguity in school") because it read as blaming K-12 teachers. Replaced with a no-blame framing: "that readiness has to start early!" — **Why:** she wants CAMEL public comms to never imply K-12 educators are at fault; frame gaps as systemic/curricular, not a teacher failing. **How to apply:** apply this same care to any future CAMEL press/social copy.
- Credits NSF and philanthropic partners (Walton Family Foundation) explicitly.
- Voice: first-person plural ("our team," "we as engineers"), enthusiastic, informal (uses "!!"), hashtags: #NSF #STEMEducation #EngineeringEducation #DataScience #K12Education.
