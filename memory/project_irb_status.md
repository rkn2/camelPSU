James Yao (Co-PI, xzy73@psu.edu) drafted the initial IRB protocol (HRP-591) for CAMEL-CN. Becca is reviewing and responding to five questions James raised before submission to PSU HRPP via CATS IRB.

**Key decisions made (as of 2026-07-09):**
- Targeting Exempt Research classification, primarily Category 1 (normal educational practices) for classroom activities, Category 2 for teacher surveys/interviews, Category 4 for the future de-identified dataset.
- Video recording of classroom sessions will be kept (not dropped to audio-only) — the research value is too high. Need to add a de-identification/destruction timeline to the protocol to protect exempt status.
- No data collection outside the regular classroom in Phase I (no task-based clinic interviews with individual students) — this keeps us cleanly in Category 1 and avoids pushing toward Expedited review.
- Section 15.3 (PI time) drafted: 33.33% AY + 1 summer month; day-to-day delegated to Co-PIs, CSATS, and grad students.

**IRB folder:** `irb/` — contains HRP-591 draft, classroom observation framework, and pre/post assessment description (both in .md and .docx).

**Still needed before submission:**
- Fix Sections 5.6.2 and 5.6.3 (parental permission/assent marked "Not applicable" but study involves minors — contradiction)
- Add video de-identification/destruction timeline to data management section
- Specify de-identification standard for secondary dataset (Safe Harbor or Expert Determination)
- Finalize pre/post assessment items (currently noted as "under development" in the instrument description)

**Why:** NSF grant requires IRB approval before data collection can begin. Phase I classroom implementation is targeted for Fall 2026.

**How to apply:** When Becca asks about IRB, assume the protocol is in pre-submission review and these open items are the priority.
