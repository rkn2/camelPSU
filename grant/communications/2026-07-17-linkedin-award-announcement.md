# LinkedIn — CAMEL Award Announcement

**Date posted:** 2026-07-17
**Related NSF announcement:** https://www.nsf.gov/sbe/updates/nsf-invests-75m-across-5-projects-enhance-k-12-mathematics

---

Excited to share that our team has been awarded a $1.5M NSF grant through the CAMEL program (Collaboratory to Advance Mathematics Education and Learning for K-12) to tackle a question we as engineers think about constantly: how do we prepare the next generation of engineers and scientists to work with data that's actually messy? Most K-12 math and data science curricula teach students on clean, tidy datasets, but that's not the world they're walking into. Real industrial and research data is high-dimensional, noisy, incomplete, and full of the kind of ambiguity that engineers navigate every day. Our project takes real engineering datasets and builds a framework to translate that complexity into high school math classrooms, without stripping out the parts that make it authentic. We're instrumenting students' actual reasoning process as they work through it: where they get stuck, how they recover, what "productive struggle" looks like. The goal is to learn how thousands of students reason through genuinely hard, high-dimensional problems and a come up with ways other engineering researchers can bring their own real-world data into the classroom.

If we want a STEM workforce that's ready for the ambiguity of real engineering problems, that readiness has to start early!

Grateful to NSF and philanthropic partners including the Walton Family Foundation for the chance to build this!!
